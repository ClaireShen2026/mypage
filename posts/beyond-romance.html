Beyond Romance

Most of my life changes so far have been driven by attachment in relationships. Romantic attraction once felt like the clearest signal in my life.

After moving out of my parents’ home and gaining autonomy in how I lived, my days became open enough that most choices felt equally acceptable. Life was stable, but without strong pressure to discover what truly suited me. In that space, attachment in relationships became the clearest direction I had.

I changed cities, routines, and entire ways of living in order to stay close to someone. I believed, without fully articulating it, that if the relationship could be sustained, life would eventually organize itself around it.

For a while, it seemed to hold.

But gradually, daily life began to test that belief.

Yesterday’s quarrel started with something simple: travel plans. She needed a break, but with rising costs and limited time, options were few. I didn’t want to spend money on a short getaway while our everyday life still felt unsteady. At the same time, I was already tired from my own rhythm—monthly trips to Hong Kong, quarterly returns to Shanghai, constant movement between places.

When our needs didn’t align, she decided to travel alone.

That should have ended the matter. But it didn’t.

The conversation shifted, quietly, into something larger—the question of how we want to live.

I said I didn’t want to stay in Guangdong for long. Only until this exploratory phase around Hong Kong settles. The climate, the humidity, the insects, the temporary apartment—all of it wears on me in a way that makes it hard to fully rest. I feel, often, slightly out of place.

She said she couldn’t afford that life.

And suddenly, what I had described as direction was heard as dissatisfaction with the present.

I stopped trying to explain.

Not because I had nothing to say—but because I didn’t know how to say it without turning a desire for another life into rejection of this one.

So I stayed quiet.

Later, I kept returning to something I used to believe: that once I understood myself better, life would begin to take shape around me.

Now it feels more like seeing a map for the first time and realizing how far things still are.

What felt close was never as close as I thought. What I am trying to build will not appear through adjustment alone. It has to be constructed slowly, from the ground.

Each person moves with their own gravity.

We are not held together by intention, but by alignment that may or may not last.

Love is not a destination. It is what sometimes appears when paths happen to overlap.

I used to believe that finding the right person would give life its shape.

Now I think life has to be shaped first.

And even then, it is not shared evenly.

The strange clarity in this is not comfort. It is direction.

I am no longer waiting for life to organize itself around attachment.

I am learning how to follow what I am drawn toward as a person—not only as someone in love.

And that, for now, is enough.
