Love Used to Move Me

Knowing myself is harder than I expected.

For a long time, love was the clearest force in my life. It was strong enough to move me out of my parents' home and build a life of my own. But looking back, that autonomy had a boundary. It separated me from being someone's daughter, yet it wasn't enough to create a new identity.

Love was different. Love gave me direction. It gave me somewhere to go, someone to move toward, and reasons to change.

Eventually, I learned something that felt obvious only in hindsight: what is near can still be unreachable. Living together, trying harder, loving deeply—none of these guarantee being truly seen.

That realization didn't provide answers. It only created questions.

What kind of life suits me? What do I value when nobody is watching? What remains when love is no longer the sole compass?

I don't think I've found those answers yet.

But learning to know myself feels like finding the first landmark on a map. The destination is still unclear. The road ahead is probably much longer than I imagine.

Still, for the first time, the movement feels like my own.
